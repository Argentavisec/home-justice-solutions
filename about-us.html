What Is Housing Disrepair?

Housing disrepair refers to any part of your rented home that is in poor condition and hasn’t been fixed by your landlord. This can impact your health, safety, and quality of life.

If you’ve already informed your landlord and given them enough time to respond but they’ve failed to act, they may be breaching their legal duties. In these cases, tenants may be entitled to:

  • Mandatory repairs
  • Financial compensation
  • Ongoing legal support

Visit our Your Rights page to better understand what your landlord is legally obligated to do under the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 and the Homes (Fitness for Human Habitation) Act 2018.

Your Legal Rights as a Tenant

Tenants have the right to live in a safe, habitable home. Landlords are legally required to carry out necessary repairs in a reasonable time. These laws apply to council tenants, housing association tenants, and private renters alike.

Common tenant rights include:

  • The right to a home free from damp and mould
  • The right to working heating, electricity, and water
  • The right to a structurally sound building
  • The right to have repairs done in a timely fashion

Visit our Your Rights page to learn more about the legal protections available to you.
