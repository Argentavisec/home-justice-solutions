Aberdeen City Council's Role in Housing Repairs and Maintenance

Aberdeen City Council has a legal duty under Scottish housing law to ensure council properties and social housing meet required health and safety standards. The council’s housing repairs department manages inspections, repairs, and enforcement to maintain safe homes.

Key responsibilities of Aberdeen City Council include:

  • Responding promptly to reports of housing disrepair and hazards
  • Inspecting council properties to identify issues like damp, mould, structural faults, and faulty heating
  • Carrying out or commissioning repairs within reasonable timescales
  • Enforcing repair obligations on private landlords where applicable
  • Providing tenant support services and advice on disrepair issues

Aberdeen City Council publishes repair standards and timescales on their official website, detailing how quickly they address common issues.

Knowing these responsibilities helps tenants hold the council accountable and make stronger disrepair claims if repairs are delayed or ignored.

Common Housing Disrepair Issues Reported to Aberdeen City Council

Tenants in Aberdeen commonly report the following housing disrepair problems to the council:

Damp and Mould Problems

Damp and mould are frequent issues due to Aberdeen’s climate and older housing stock. These conditions can cause respiratory problems, allergies, and damage to walls and furnishings.

Heating and Hot Water Failures

Broken boilers, radiators, and hot water systems leave tenants without adequate heating during Aberdeen’s cold winters, affecting comfort and health.

Leaks and Water Damage

Roof leaks and faulty plumbing cause water damage to ceilings, walls, and floors, increasing repair costs and safety risks.

Electrical Faults and Hazards

Faulty wiring and outdated electrical systems are hazardous and can result in shocks, fires, or failure of essential appliances.

Structural Problems and Subsidence

Cracks, subsidence, and foundation issues threaten property safety and require professional surveys and urgent repair.

Pest Infestations

Rodents, insects, or other infestations often stem from poor maintenance and must be addressed promptly.

How to Report Housing Disrepair to Aberdeen City Council

Prompt reporting is vital to ensure repairs are scheduled and completed. To report disrepair to Aberdeen City Council:

  1. Visit the official Aberdeen City Council website and use their online repair reporting form.
  2. Call the council housing repairs hotline for urgent issues.
  3. Send a written complaint detailing the issues, ideally including photos or videos.
  4. Keep copies of all communications with the council and repair notices.

Clearly document the nature of the problem, its location in the property, and any health or safety impacts. For emergencies like electrical hazards, report immediately via phone.

Aberdeen City Council aims to respond within defined timescales based on the severity of the disrepair.

Your Legal Rights as a Tenant with Aberdeen City Council

Scottish housing law protects tenants in Aberdeen against poor housing conditions. Your rights include:

  • Right to live in a property meeting the Repairing Standard
  • Right to have repairs carried out within a reasonable timeframe
  • Right to compensation for health issues or property damage caused by disrepair
  • Protection from eviction or harassment when reporting disrepair
  • Right to escalate complaints to the Scottish Housing Regulator if the council fails to act

Understanding these rights helps you hold Aberdeen City Council accountable and supports your legal claim if necessary.

Gathering Evidence to Support Your Aberdeen City Council Housing Disrepair Claim

Evidence is crucial for a successful claim. Collect the following:

  • Clear, dated photos or videos showing all disrepair issues
  • Copies of written reports or emails sent to Aberdeen City Council
  • Medical records linking health problems to housing conditions
  • Expert surveyor or environmental health officer reports
  • Receipts for alternative accommodation or repairs you arranged
  • Witness statements from neighbours or healthcare professionals

Well-organised evidence strengthens your case and can increase compensation awards.

Compensation for Housing Disrepair Claims in Aberdeen

Compensation amounts vary based on disrepair severity, health impact, and duration. Typical awards cover:

  • Inconvenience and distress caused by poor housing conditions
  • Medical costs related to disrepair-induced health issues
  • Damage or loss of personal belongings
  • Temporary accommodation costs if you had to relocate
  • Legal and expert fees where applicable

In Aberdeen, claims can range from a few hundred pounds for minor issues to several thousand for serious or prolonged disrepair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making a Housing Disrepair Claim with Aberdeen City Council

  1. Report the disrepair promptly to Aberdeen City Council in writing and keep records.
  2. Allow the council reasonable time to carry out repairs according to their standards.
  3. If ignored or delayed, seek expert legal advice from specialists in Aberdeen housing law.
  4. Your solicitor will investigate, gather evidence, and prepare a formal letter of claim.
  5. Attempt negotiation or alternative dispute resolution (ADR) to settle without court.
  6. If necessary, take the claim to court or housing tribunal.
  7. Obtain court orders for repair and/or compensation if successful.

Early action and good record-keeping significantly improve your chances of success.
