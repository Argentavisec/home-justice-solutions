Supporting Tenants Through the Breathing Space Initiative

We are proud to support the UK government's Breathing Space scheme, which offers legal protection for tenants struggling with debt or financial difficulties, including those affected by housing disrepair. This initiative provides a 60-day period where creditors must pause enforcement actions, giving tenants much-needed time to seek help and organise their affairs.

Our team advises on how Breathing Space applies to housing disrepair situations, helping you protect your rights while you pursue repairs and compensation. We ensure you fully understand the legal safeguards available during this critical time.

Understanding Your Housing Disrepair Rights

Every tenant in the UK has the right to live in a safe and habitable home. Landlords are legally obligated to maintain their properties and carry out repairs promptly. Our role is to educate you about these rights and help you enforce them.

Housing disrepair can affect your health, safety, and quality of life. We explain what qualifies as disrepair, your landlord's duties, and the legal framework supporting tenants, including the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 and the Homes (Fitness for Human Habitation) Act 2018.

We guide you through recognising disrepair issues, reporting them correctly, and what to do if your landlord neglects repairs.

Find Your Local Council for Housing Disrepair Support

Local councils play a crucial role in enforcing housing standards. If your landlord fails to carry out essential repairs, the council’s housing enforcement officers can inspect your property and issue legal notices requiring landlords to act.

We help you identify and contact the right council department for your area, ensuring timely inspections and enforcement actions when needed.

Understanding your council’s powers can increase pressure on landlords and speed up repair resolutions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Housing Disrepair Claims

We know housing disrepair claims can be confusing, so we have compiled answers to the most common questions:

  • Can I claim compensation if my landlord ignores repairs? Yes, tenants can pursue claims for inconvenience, distress, and property damage.
  • What evidence do I need? Detailed photos, communication records, medical reports if applicable, and council inspection documents.
  • How long does a claim take? Typically 6 months to 2 years, depending on complexity.
  • Will claiming affect my future renting? No legal negative impact if handled correctly.
  • Can I make a claim if I no longer live there? Yes, under certain conditions.
