What Is Housing Disrepair?

Housing disrepair refers to situations where your rented property has structural or maintenance issues that your landlord has failed to fix, even after being notified. Under UK law, landlords are responsible for ensuring the home is:

  • Safe and free from hazards
  • Structurally sound
  • Free of leaks, mould, and damp
  • Properly heated and ventilated
  • Free from pests and infestations

If your landlord refuses to repair issues after you've reported them, you may be legally entitled to start a housing disrepair compensation claim.

Common Issues That Qualify for a Claim

You may qualify for a disrepair claim if your home has:

  • Mould or damp that affects your health
  • Leaks or water damage from the roof, windows or pipes
  • Broken heating systems or no hot water
  • Unsafe electrics or exposed wires
  • Cracked walls, structural issues or falling ceilings
  • Infestations of rats, mice, cockroaches or bedbugs
  • Blocked drains or sewage issues

Even if you’re still living in the property, you may be entitled to financial compensation — often worth £1,000–£10,000+ depending on the damage and how long it’s been ignored.
