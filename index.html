What Is Housing Disrepair?

Housing disrepair refers to problems in your rented property that your landlord hasn’t fixed, despite being informed. This includes:

  • Black mould or excessive damp
  • Leaks or water damage from the roof or plumbing
  • Broken heating, radiators or boilers
  • Unsafe electrics or exposed wires
  • Cracks, crumbling plaster or structural damage
  • Infestations of rats, mice or insects

These issues can affect your health, safety, and quality of life — and you don’t have to put up with them.

Do I Qualify for a Disrepair Claim?

You may be eligible if:

  • You’re a council tenant or rent from a housing association
  • You've reported the issues but your landlord has not resolved them
  • You or your family have been affected physically, mentally, or financially

Compensation amounts can range from £1,000–£10,000+ depending on the severity and duration of the disrepair.
