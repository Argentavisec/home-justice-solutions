Adur District Council’s Role and Legal Responsibilities in Housing Repairs

Adur District Council, as a landlord of social and council housing, is legally required under the Housing Act 2004 and other regulations to maintain properties to the Decent Homes Standard and ensure they are safe, warm, and free from health hazards.

The Council’s housing repairs department is responsible for:

  • Responding promptly to tenant reports of disrepair and hazards
  • Inspecting properties and identifying defects such as damp, mould, heating failures, and structural faults
  • Arranging repairs within specified timescales, prioritising urgent and emergency works
  • Enforcing repair obligations on private landlords where applicable
  • Providing advice and support services to tenants facing disrepair

The Council publishes clear repair timescales, e.g., emergency heating repairs are typically resolved within 24 hours, urgent repairs within 7 days, and routine repairs within 28 days.

If the Council fails in its duties, tenants have the right to escalate complaints to the Housing Ombudsman or seek legal enforcement and compensation through the courts.

Common Housing Disrepair Issues Reported to Adur District Council

Tenants in the Adur District frequently face the following disrepair problems:

Damp and Mould

Dampness and mould are common due to the coastal climate, poor ventilation, and older housing stock. Persistent damp causes wall and floor damage and aggravates respiratory illnesses like asthma and bronchitis.

Heating and Hot Water Failures

Malfunctioning boilers, radiators, and hot water systems often leave tenants without adequate heating, especially during cold months, severely affecting quality of life.

Leaks and Water Damage

Roof leaks, broken gutters, and plumbing faults lead to water ingress that damages ceilings, walls, and flooring, potentially causing structural decay.

Electrical Faults and Hazards

Outdated or faulty wiring, exposed cables, and defective appliances pose serious fire and electric shock risks that require immediate attention.

Structural Problems

Issues like wall cracks, subsidence, or unstable flooring threaten tenant safety and require professional assessment and urgent repairs.

Pest Infestations

Rodents and insects often invade homes suffering neglect or poor maintenance, creating health and hygiene concerns.

How to Report Housing Disrepair to Adur District Council

Reporting disrepair quickly and accurately is essential for ensuring repairs are done promptly and to build evidence for any claim. Follow these steps:

  1. Submit an online repair request using Adur District Council’s official website for non-urgent issues.
  2. Call the emergency repairs hotline immediately for urgent hazards like no heating or electrical dangers.
  3. Follow up with a detailed written complaint (email or letter), describing the issue, its location, and effects, including photos or videos if possible.
  4. Keep copies of all communication and any official responses or repair appointments.

Be sure to clearly state how the disrepair impacts your health, safety, or quality of life to emphasise urgency.

If repairs are delayed or not made, escalate using the Council’s formal complaints procedure or seek legal advice.

Gathering Evidence to Support Your Adur District Council Housing Disrepair Claim

A strong housing disrepair claim requires solid evidence. Collect the following:

  • Dated photographs and videos clearly showing all affected areas
  • Copies of all repair requests, emails, letters sent to the Council
  • Responses and repair schedules provided by the Council
  • Medical reports connecting health problems to housing conditions
  • Independent surveyor or environmental health inspection reports
  • Receipts for temporary accommodation or repairs you paid for yourself
  • Witness statements from neighbours, family, or healthcare professionals

Organising your evidence chronologically and clearly will strengthen your solicitor’s ability to represent you effectively.

Frequently Asked Questions About Adur District Council Housing Disrepair Claims

What qualifies as housing disrepair?

Housing disrepair covers defects or neglect affecting your home's safety, health, or comfort, including damp, mould, leaks, faulty heating or electrics, structural damage, and pests.

How do I report housing disrepair to Adur District Council?

Report via the Council’s online repair form, emergency hotline for urgent issues, or written complaints with photos or videos.

How long does the Council take to fix repairs?

Timescales depend on urgency; emergencies are addressed within 24 hours, urgent repairs within 7 days, and routine repairs within 28 days.

Can I withhold rent if repairs are not done?

Withholding rent without advice is risky and may cause eviction. Instead, seek legal advice or use rent escrow schemes under guidance.

What compensation can I claim?

Compensation may include damages for inconvenience, health costs, property damage, temporary accommodation, and legal fees.

Do I need a solicitor?

You can claim independently but solicitors increase success chances by managing evidence and legal processes.

What if the Council ignores my requests?

Escalate complaints to the Housing Ombudsman or seek legal advice.

Can private tenants claim through the Council?

Yes, for enforcement against negligent landlords; direct claims against landlords are also possible.

How long do I have to make a claim?

The limitation period in England is generally six years from the date you knew of the disrepair.

What is Alternative Dispute Resolution?

ADR includes mediation and arbitration, providing quicker, less adversarial dispute resolution than courts.
