Aberdeen City Council Housing Disrepair Claims

Are you living in an Aberdeen City Council property that’s in poor condition? Struggling with asthma or illness from damp and mould? Leaks, faulty electrics, broken heating or structural issues could all be signs of serious housing disrepair — and you may be eligible to claim compensation.

At Home Justice Solutions, we help Aberdeen council tenants like you take legal action against poor housing conditions. We work with specialist housing solicitors who operate on a no win, no fee basis, so you pay nothing upfront. Your health, safety, and comfort in your home matter — and the council has a legal duty to maintain livable standards under the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985.

Compensation payouts often range from hundreds to thousands of pounds, especially in cases involving long-standing neglect, repeated complaints, or health damage.

What Can I Claim Compensation For?

If Aberdeen City Council has failed to repair your home despite repeated complaints or delays, you may be entitled to claim for:

  • Respiratory illnesses caused by mould or damp (e.g., asthma, chest infections)
  • Damage to belongings such as furniture, clothing or electricals from leaks
  • Stress, inconvenience and discomfort caused by prolonged disrepair
  • Temporary accommodation or relocation costs (in severe cases)

We also help you collect evidence to support your claim — such as medical records, photos of damage, complaint logs, and housing inspection reports.

Common Issues Reported in Aberdeen City Council Properties

Aberdeen residents regularly report serious maintenance problems in council homes. These include:

  • Black mould spreading in bedrooms, kitchens, and bathrooms
  • Leaking roofs, gutters or internal pipes damaging ceilings and carpets
  • Broken boilers and radiators leaving homes without proper heating
  • Cracked windows and poor insulation leading to unsafe temperatures
  • Vermin infestations due to structural gaps and disrepair

If any of these issues apply to your home — and Aberdeen City Council has failed to fix them after you’ve complained — you could be entitled to claim.
