Aberdeen City Council’s Role and Legal Responsibilities in Housing Repairs

Aberdeen City Council holds significant legal responsibilities under Scottish housing law, especially the Housing (Scotland) Act 2006. This legislation requires council landlords to ensure that all social and council housing meets the Repairing Standard, which mandates that properties must be:

  • Wind and watertight
  • Structurally stable
  • Equipped with safe and effective electrical, heating, and plumbing systems
  • Free from dampness and mould
  • Properly maintained to avoid health and safety risks

To comply, the Council operates a dedicated housing repairs department responsible for:

  • Responding promptly to repair requests and reports of hazards
  • Conducting regular property inspections and health & safety audits
  • Carrying out repairs or commissioning contractors within legally required timescales
  • Enforcing repair standards on private landlords under the Repairing Standard Enforcement Order where applicable
  • Providing tenants with support and advice services regarding housing disrepair issues

Official Council repair standards define timescales, such as emergency heating repairs to be completed within 24 hours, urgent repairs within days, and routine fixes within weeks. Despite these legal requirements, tenants sometimes face delays due to budget or staffing constraints, making it essential to understand how to escalate complaints and protect your rights.

If Aberdeen City Council fails to meet its duties, tenants can seek intervention from the Scottish Housing Regulator, which has powers to investigate, impose sanctions, and demand improvement.

Common Housing Disrepair Issues Reported to Aberdeen City Council

Across Aberdeen, tenants report a variety of disrepair problems, including:

Damp and Mould Problems

Damp and mould are prevalent issues due to Aberdeen’s wet climate and older housing construction. Persistent dampness arises from leaking roofs, defective gutters, rising damp, or condensation caused by poor ventilation. Mould spores produced from these conditions are known triggers for asthma, bronchitis, allergies, and other respiratory illnesses.

Heating and Hot Water Failures

Broken boilers, malfunctioning radiators, or leaking hot water pipes leave tenants without adequate heating during cold winters. This impacts comfort, health, and increases risks for vulnerable groups such as children and the elderly.

Leaks and Water Damage

Leaking roofs, broken plumbing fixtures, and burst pipes cause water to penetrate ceilings, walls, and floors. This damages structural elements and can cause electrical hazards if water reaches wiring.

Electrical Faults and Hazards

Faulty wiring, outdated fuse boxes, exposed cables, or defective appliances create serious fire and shock risks. These electrical defects are dangerous and must be remedied urgently.

Structural Problems and Subsidence

Cracks in walls or foundations, subsidence, or unstable flooring jeopardise the safety of tenants and often require expert survey and urgent repairs.

Pest Infestations

Rodents, insects, and other pests may invade homes due to neglected repairs, creating hygiene risks and property damage.

How to Report Housing Disrepair to Aberdeen City Council

Prompt and detailed reporting helps ensure that repair issues are addressed quickly and builds a record useful for compensation claims. Follow these steps:

  1. Online Reporting: Use Aberdeen City Council’s official website repair reporting form for non-urgent issues. Save confirmation emails or take screenshots.
  2. Emergency Hotline: For urgent problems such as no heating in winter or electrical hazards, call the council’s emergency repairs line immediately.
  3. Written Complaint: Follow up with a written letter or email outlining the issues in detail. Include dates, descriptions, and photographic or video evidence if possible.
  4. Keep Copies: Retain copies of all correspondence, responses, and repair schedules. Record dates and times of communications.

Be clear about the impact on your health, safety, and living conditions to emphasise urgency. If repairs are delayed or ignored, escalate through the council’s formal complaints procedure or seek advice from tenant advocacy groups.

Your Legal Rights as a Tenant with Aberdeen City Council

Scottish tenants enjoy robust legal protections designed to ensure safe, habitable housing. These rights include:

  • Right to a Home Meeting the Repairing Standard: Your home must be wind and watertight, structurally sound, with safe electrical and heating systems.
  • Right to Timely Repairs: Repairs must be carried out within reasonable timescales as outlined by Aberdeen City Council.
  • Right to Compensation: You may claim for physical injury, emotional distress, or property damage caused by disrepair.
  • Protection Against Eviction or Harassment: It is illegal for landlords or councils to evict or harass tenants for reporting disrepair.
  • Right to Escalate Complaints: If issues remain unresolved, you can complain to the Scottish Housing Regulator or pursue claims at housing tribunals.

Familiarising yourself with these rights empowers you to insist on action and safeguards you from unfair treatment.

Gathering Evidence to Support Your Aberdeen City Council Housing Disrepair Claim

The strength of your housing disrepair claim depends heavily on the quality and organisation of your evidence. Important items include:

  • Clear, dated photographs and videos showing the disrepair in detail
  • Copies of repair requests, emails, letters sent to Aberdeen City Council
  • Written council responses, repair schedules, and appointment confirmations
  • Medical reports or letters linking health problems (e.g. asthma, dermatitis) to housing conditions
  • Independent surveyor or environmental health officer inspection reports
  • Receipts or invoices for temporary accommodation or repairs you paid for personally
  • Witness statements from neighbours, family, or health professionals corroborating conditions or health effects

Carefully organising these documents chronologically helps solicitors assess and present your case more effectively, increasing chances of compensation.

  1. Report the Disrepair: Formally notify Aberdeen City Council in writing and keep detailed records of all communications.
  2. Allow Reasonable Time: Permit the council time to carry out repairs as per their published standards.
  3. Seek Expert Legal Advice: If ignored or delayed, consult solicitors specialising in Aberdeen housing law for guidance.
  4. Investigation and Letter of Claim: Your solicitor will gather evidence, investigate, and draft a formal letter of claim to the Council.
  5. Negotiation and ADR: Attempt to resolve disputes through negotiation or Alternative Dispute Resolution (mediation/arbitration) to avoid court.
  6. Court or Tribunal Proceedings: If unresolved, pursue a hearing before housing tribunals or courts.
  7. Obtain Orders and Compensation: Secure court orders requiring repairs and awards of compensation if successful.

Throughout, good record keeping, prompt action, and legal support improve chances of a favourable outcome.

Frequently Asked Questions About Aberdeen City Council Housing Disrepair Claims

What qualifies as housing disrepair?

Housing disrepair includes any defects or neglect that negatively affect the safety, health, or comfort of your home. This covers damp, mould, leaks, faulty heating or electrical systems, structural damage, broken fixtures, and pest infestations.

How do I report housing disrepair to Aberdeen City Council?

You can report via the Council’s online repair form, phone their housing repairs hotline for emergencies, or send a detailed written complaint with photos or videos.

How long does the Council take to fix repairs?

Repair times depend on urgency. Emergency repairs typically occur within 24 hours, urgent repairs within days, and routine repairs within weeks. The Council publishes detailed repair timescales.

Can I withhold rent if repairs are not done?

Withholding rent without legal advice is risky and may lead to eviction. It’s better to document issues and seek legal guidance or use rent escrow schemes under professional advice.

What compensation can I claim?

Compensation covers inconvenience, health costs, damaged belongings, temporary accommodation, and sometimes legal fees, depending on case severity.

Do I need a solicitor to make a claim?

You can claim yourself, but solicitors improve success chances by managing evidence, negotiations, and legal procedures.

What if the Council ignores my requests?

Escalate via formal complaints, tenant support groups, the Scottish Housing Regulator, or seek legal help.

Can private tenants claim with Aberdeen City Council?

Yes, private tenants can seek Council enforcement if landlords fail repairs and pursue claims directly against landlords.

How long do I have to make a claim?

In Scotland, the limitation period is generally five years from when you became aware of the disrepair.

What is Alternative Dispute Resolution?

ADR includes mediation and arbitration, providing faster, less adversarial dispute resolution than courts.
