What Counts as Housing Disrepair?

Housing disrepair refers to serious maintenance issues that affect your comfort, health, or safety. These problems must have been reported to the council or landlord and left unresolved for a significant period.

  • Black mould or damp causing breathing problems
  • Leaky pipes, roofs, or windows leading to water damage
  • Faulty heating or no hot water
  • Broken electrics, unsafe stairs or floors
  • Rodent infestations or structural damage

If you're experiencing any of these, especially if it’s affecting your health, belongings, or peace of mind, you're entitled to take legal action.

Health Risks from Mould & Damp in Angus Homes

Many families across Angus suffer from ongoing health problems due to black mould, condensation, and cold, damp homes. Common issues include:

  • Worsening of asthma and respiratory issues
  • Skin rashes, eye irritation, headaches
  • Children falling ill more frequently

Disrepair isn’t just inconvenient — it’s dangerous. If your council housing has made you or your family ill, you may be entitled to medical-related compensation in addition to general damages.
