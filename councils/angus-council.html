Understanding Your Rights as a Tenant

As a tenant in a council-owned property, you have a legal right to live in a safe, well-maintained home. If Angus Council fails to respond to your repair requests within a reasonable timeframe, they may be in breach of their responsibilities under the Housing (Scotland) Act 2006. You do not need to suffer in silence. Disrepair can impact your health, safety, and overall quality of life—and legal action can result in:

  • Financial compensation for inconvenience and hardship
  • Repairs being completed quickly
  • Reimbursement for damage to belongings
  • Compensation for health-related issues caused by poor housing conditions

If you’re unsure whether you qualify, you can explore what types of claims you can make on our website.

Common Housing Disrepair Issues in Angus

Through our work across Scotland, we’ve seen recurring housing issues reported by Angus Council tenants. These problems are often ignored or delayed by the council, leaving tenants in unsafe conditions. Some of the most common disrepair issues include:

  • Black mould and damp caused by poor ventilation and leaks
  • Faulty or broken heating systems in winter
  • Unsafe electrics and exposed wiring
  • Leaking roofs or windows leading to water damage
  • Structural damage, including cracks, loose tiles, or collapsing ceilings
  • Pest infestations due to broken seals or poor hygiene from neighbouring properties

If you’ve already reported one or more of these issues and the council has failed to act within a reasonable period, you may be entitled to claim. Take the first step today by using our quick online form.
