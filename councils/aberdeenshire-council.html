Aberdeenshire Council Housing Disrepair Claims

If you rent your home from Aberdeenshire Council and are living in poor housing conditions due to damp, mould, leaks, broken heating, faulty electrics, or structural problems, you may be eligible for housing disrepair compensation. At Home Justice Solutions, we help tenants across Aberdeenshire claim what they're rightfully owed when the council fails in its duty of care.

Aberdeenshire Council, like all local authorities, has a legal obligation to maintain its housing stock. When essential repairs are ignored — even after repeated reports — tenants suffer both physically and financially. If this sounds familiar, we can help you take action today.

What Counts as Housing Disrepair in Aberdeenshire?

Tenants across Stonehaven, Fraserburgh, Peterhead, Inverurie, and other Aberdeenshire towns frequently report problems such as:

  • Black mould spreading in bathrooms and bedrooms
  • Persistent condensation and damp due to poor ventilation
  • Leaking roofs, gutters, or pipes causing water damage
  • Broken boilers or lack of central heating in winter
  • Faulty electrics or dangerous wiring
  • Cracks in walls, rotting windows, or collapsing ceilings

These are not just minor issues — they can cause serious health risks, especially for children, the elderly, or those with existing medical conditions.

Your Legal Rights as a Council Tenant in Aberdeenshire

Under the Housing (Scotland) Act 2001, Aberdeenshire Council must keep your home in a reasonable state of repair. If you've reported an issue and the council hasn’t fixed it in a reasonable time, you can bring a claim for compensation.

You also have a right to live in a safe, warm, and watertight home — and to be free from the stress, discomfort, and property damage caused by long-term disrepair.

For additional help on tenant rights, you can visit Shelter Scotland.

What Can You Claim Compensation For?

Depending on your situation, you may be entitled to claim compensation for:

  • Health issues caused by mould, damp or cold
  • Damage to belongings (clothes, furniture, electronics)
  • Disruption and inconvenience
  • Cost of alternative accommodation (if applicable)
  • Loss of use of part of your home

In many cases, tenants have received £1,000 – £10,000 or more in compensation. If you've suffered due to inaction from Aberdeenshire Council, you may be entitled too.
