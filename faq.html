Frequently Asked Questions – Housing Disrepair Claims

Have questions about starting a disrepair claim? You're not alone. Below we’ve answered some of the most common questions tenants ask when dealing with poor housing conditions and negligent landlords.

What is a housing disrepair claim?

A housing disrepair claim is a legal process where tenants hold their landlord accountable for failing to fix serious problems in their rented property — such as mould, leaks, broken heating or infestations. If the landlord has ignored repair requests, you may be entitled to free repairs and financial compensation.

What types of disrepair can I claim for?

You can claim for many issues, including:

  • Black mould or damp
  • Leaks and water damage
  • Broken heating or radiators
  • Unsafe electrics
  • Infestations (rats, mice, cockroaches)
  • Structural problems like cracked walls or ceilings

See our full Claim Types page for more details.

Am I eligible to make a claim?

You likely qualify if:

  • You rent from a council or housing association
  • You've reported the disrepair to your landlord
  • The landlord has failed to fix the issue within a reasonable time

How much compensation can I receive?

Compensation varies based on the severity and duration of the disrepair. In general, tenants may receive between £1,000 to £10,000+. This includes money for distress, inconvenience, health effects, and loss of use of your home.

Is there a cost to make a claim?

No. All claims we help with are done on a no win, no fee basis. You pay nothing up front, and nothing at all unless your case is successful.

Will I have to go to court?

In most cases, no. Many disrepair claims are settled out of court. If your case does go to court, your solicitor will handle everything for you.

What if my landlord threatens eviction?

It's illegal for your landlord to evict or intimidate you for making a disrepair claim. You have strong legal protections as a tenant. Our partners can advise you if this happens.

How long does the process take?

Most disrepair claims are resolved within 3–6 months. Complex cases may take longer, but you'll be kept informed throughout the process.
