    Why It’s Important to Know Your Council

    Every local authority in the UK has a legal duty to maintain safe and habitable homes for its tenants. If you're living in social housing or council-provided accommodation, your local council is responsible for ensuring that repairs are handled promptly and that your living conditions meet health and safety standards. Issues like mould, damp, leaks, broken heating, or structural damage are not just inconveniences — they may be considered legal disrepair.

    By finding your council using our search tool, you can take the first step toward holding them accountable and protecting your rights as a tenant. Learn how housing disrepair claims work here.

    How Housing Disrepair Affects Your Health

    Living with damp, mould, or broken facilities can have a serious impact on your wellbeing. Many tenants suffer from respiratory issues, anxiety, and stress due to unresolved disrepair. Councils and housing associations must address these risks — and if they don’t, you may be eligible to claim compensation or force the repairs to be done.

    Common symptoms of housing disrepair include:

    • Persistent cold and condensation inside the home
    • Mould-related breathing problems or allergies
    • Leaks causing damage to walls, ceilings or electrics
    • Lack of hot water or heating during cold months

    Can I Still Claim If I’m On Benefits?

    Yes. Being on Universal Credit, Housing Benefit, or other forms of support does not stop you from making a housing disrepair claim. In fact, many people in social housing are eligible. These claims are usually no-win, no-fee, meaning you don’t need to pay anything upfront to begin the process.

    Whether you’re on a low income or out of work, you still deserve a safe home. Read our FAQs for more help on claiming while on benefits.

    What to Do If Your Council Isn’t Fixing Repairs

    If your council isn’t taking action, document all communication and evidence of disrepair. Take photos, keep a repair diary, and save complaint emails or letters. Then check if you qualify to make a claim. You may be able to claim for:

    • Respiratory illnesses from mould or damp
    • Damage to personal belongings from leaks
    • Stress, inconvenience, or unsafe conditions
    • Relocation costs if your home becomes uninhabitable
