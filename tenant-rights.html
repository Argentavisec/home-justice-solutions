Your Rights as a Tenant

If you rent from a council or housing association, you have legal rights that protect you from poor living conditions. At Home Justice Solutions, we help tenants across the UK take action when landlords ignore necessary repairs.

Whether you’re dealing with black mould, leaks, broken heating or structural damage, the law is on your side — and we’re here to make sure your voice is heard.

What Are My Legal Rights as a Tenant?

As a tenant, your landlord is legally required to maintain your property in a safe, livable condition. This is known as the landlord’s duty of care. If your landlord has failed to carry out repairs after you've reported them, they may be in breach of the tenancy agreement and housing laws.

You have the right to live in a home that is:

  • Free from damp, mould, or infestations
  • Structurally sound with no dangerous hazards
  • Properly heated with working boilers or radiators
  • Well maintained with functioning plumbing and electrics

If your home falls below this standard, you may be entitled to repairs and financial compensation.

What Should I Do If My Landlord Ignores Repairs?

If you’ve already reported the disrepair to your landlord or housing association and nothing has been done, you have the right to escalate the issue. You can:

  • Document the problem with photos and written complaints
  • Report it again and request a formal response
  • Start a disrepair claim if they continue to ignore you

With Home Justice Solutions, you don’t need to call or visit anyone — just fill out our 60-second online form and we’ll connect you with legal specialists who can help you take action.

What Can I Claim For?

If your landlord is found responsible for neglecting repairs, you may be able to claim for:

  • The cost of damaged belongings (e.g. from leaks or mould)
  • Inconvenience caused by living in unsafe or unhealthy conditions
  • Health impacts caused by mould, damp or cold temperatures
  • Rent refunds for time spent in unfit conditions

Each case is different, but compensation claims typically range from £1,000 to £10,000+ depending on the severity of the issue.
